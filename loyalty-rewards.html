Launching Loyalty:
Omnichannel Rewards Program

My Role: Product Design  ·  Research  ·  UI Design  ·  UX Design  ·  UX Writing   |   Live since: November 2024   |   Markets: 13 European markets

Overview

I led the end-to-end design of an omnichannel loyalty program that unifies retail and online shopping for 3+ fashion brands across 13 European markets. Delivered a single member experience on web, app, and POS that increased sign-ups and incremental revenue in its first post-launch quarter.

Why Did We Do It?

We needed to replace fragmented brand clubs with a unified loyalty platform to address four core business priorities:

  • Increase customer lifetime value and repeat purchases
  • Capture GDPR-compliant, cross-channel first-party data
  • Support the company's Unified Commerce strategy
  • Differentiate our brands in a crowded fashion market

Key Features

  • Unified Dashboard — Consolidates orders, returns, and reward activity across web, app, and store.
  • Offline-Capable Member ID — Scannable QR / barcode that instantly recognises members at any POS, even without network connectivity.
  • GDPR-Compliant Sign-Up — Captures explicit marketing consent and offers a self-serve preference centre.
  • Near-Real-Time Points Ledger — Updates balances within minutes of any transaction or return.
  • Cross-Channel Welcome Perk — Automatic 10% voucher redeemable on the first purchase online or in store.
  • Fixed-Value Reward Vouchers — €5 / €10 / €20 vouchers unlocked at predefined point milestones, stackable per checkout.

Competitive Analysis

Benchmarked H&M Plus, Just Eat Takeaway, and Etos across tier mechanics & rewards, voucher redemption across channels, and real-time in-store integrations.

User Flows

Mapped the key journeys covering every touchpoint — from first awareness to habitual engagement:

  • Entry points (homepage, PDP, checkout, store signage)
  • Sign in / sign up (social, email, SMS)
  • Earning & redeeming rewards
  • Post-purchase engagement (push, email, in-app)
